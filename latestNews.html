Teenagers set fire to Russian military helicopter in Omsk

On September 21, two teenagers in Omsk, Russia, set fire to a Mi-8 helicopter at an airbase using a Molotov cocktail, as reported by the Telegram channel Baza. The 16-year-olds were detained and claimed they were offered $20,000 via Telegram for the attack. The helicopter sustained significant damage. This incident follows a similar attack on September 11, when two younger boys ignited a Mi-8 helicopter at Noyabrsk airport in the Tyumen region. Additionally, various sabotage incidents, including train derailments, have occurred throughout Russia. In January, Ukraine's military intelligence agency (HUR) reported that some railway tracks were targeted by "unknown opponents of Putin's regime."

Helicopter goes missing in far eastern Russia with 22 people on board

A helicopter carrying 22 people, mostly tourists, has gone missing in Russia’s Kamchatka peninsula. Authorities reported losing contact with the Mi-8 helicopter at 04:15 GMT. It had 19 passengers and three crew members on board, according to Governor Vladimir Solodov in a Telegram video posted on Saturday.
