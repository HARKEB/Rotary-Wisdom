Mi-26

The Mil Mi-26 is the world's largest helicopter, designed in the Soviet Union and first entering service in 1980. It was engineered primarily for heavy-lift transport, capable of carrying up to 20 tons of cargo. Its distinctive design features a wide fuselage and twin rotors, allowing for exceptional stability and lifting capability.

Ka-27

The Ka-27 is a Russian naval helicopter designed for anti-submarine warfare (ASW) and search-and-rescue (SAR) operations. Equipped with a distinctive coaxial rotor system, it performs well in maritime environments, offering stability in high winds and confined spaces, like ship decks. The Ka-27 is widely used by the Russian Navy and other international forces for reconnaissance, transport, and rescue missions at sea.

Mil-V-12

The Mil V-12, also known as the Mil Mi-12, is a heavy-lift helicopter developed by the Soviet Union in the 1960s. Notable for its massive size and twin-rotor design, the V-12 was intended to transport large cargo loads and support military operations. It features a distinctive fuselage, capable of carrying up to 30 tons, making it one of the heaviest helicopters ever built.
