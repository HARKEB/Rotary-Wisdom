Ka-50

The development of the helicopter was long. The program started in 1984, but the first prototype Ka-60-01 flew in December 1998, and the second in 2007. The Ka-50 "Black Shark" is a Russian single-seat attack helicopter featuring a unique coaxial rotor system and heavy armor. It’s highly maneuverable and equipped with powerful weapons, making it effective for anti-armor missions and close air support in challenging environments.

Mi-28

The Mil Mi-28, also known as the "Havoc," is a Russian attack helicopter built for all-weather, day-and-night combat. It features advanced armor, a tandem cockpit, and powerful armaments, including a 30mm cannon and anti-tank missiles, designed for engaging armored targets and providing close air support. Known for its agility and durability, the Mi-28 excels in rough environments and challenging missions.

Mi-8

The Mil Mi-8 is a versatile twin-engine helicopter developed by the Soviet Union, first flying in 1961. Initially designed for transport and utility roles, it quickly gained recognition for its reliability and adaptability in various environments. The Mi-8 was used extensively during the Cold War for troop transport, medevac, and cargo missions.
